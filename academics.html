1. Estimation of Carbon Absorption by Plant Biomass Using Sentinel-2 Images (Case Study: Yogyakarta City)

Vegetation Density Map

Sample Point Map

The increasing human activities on the earth's surface contribute to significant greenhouse gas emissions in the atmosphere, leading to temperature changes. Changes in land use from green spaces to residential or industrial areas are a primary source of these emissions, especially in urban regions. Plants, with their biomass, play a vital role in absorbing carbon‚Äîthe largest component of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. This study aimed to estimate carbon absorption by plant biomass in Yogyakarta City, a region vulnerable to land conversion due to rapid population growth. The research utilized Sentinel-2 imagery, with biomass distribution derived from regression analysis between NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) and field biomass measurements.

    2. WANAGIS (WANAGAMA WebGIS): WebGIS Dissemination of Forest Resources in Wanagama

    WANAGIS

    Map View - WANAGIS

    WANAGIS is a WebGIS developed for the dissemination of forest resource data in Wanagama, a Special Purpose Forest Area managed by Universitas Gadjah Mada. Wanagama serves multiple purposes, including reforestation, education, nature tourism, and community events.
    Development Tools and Technologies:

  • Frontend: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, OpenLayers
  • Backend: Laravel Framework, XAMPP(local server)
  • Database: PostgreSQL
  • GIS Software:QGIS for spatial data preprocessing
  • Mapping Library: OpenLayers

    • Features of WANAGIS:
  • Layer control
  • Line and area measurements
  • Pop-up information
  • Attribute tables
  • Data export and map printing
  • User management
  • Layer editing and management

    • The user-friendly interface was designed with a user-centered approach to enhance accessibility and usability. WANAGIS displays forest resource data, such as plot boundaries, slope, soil type, land cover, roads, rivers, and infrastructure.

    Significance: WANAGIS optimizes the dissemination and utilization of forestry data, supporting sustainable forest management in Wanagama.

    3. Analysis of Land Deforestation in Sintang Regency (2009-2021)

    Peta Tingkat Deforestasi di Kabupaten Sintang

    Peta Deforestasi pada Kawasan Hutan di Kabupaten Sintang

    Sintang Regency faces significant challenges due to increasing deforestation driven by land-use changes. This study, conducted during an internship at Karomap Semesta, analyzed deforestation trends to measure forest cover changes from 2009 to 2021.

    Peta Tutupan Hutan di Luar Kawasan Hutan Kabupaten Sintang

    Findings:

  • Total deforestation: 100,528.41 hectares
  • Major contributors to deforestation:
    • Conversion to plantations: 38.19%
    • Dryland farming mixed with shrubs: 32.71%
    • Shrubland: 18.56%
  • Forest cover decline:
    • From 1,019,897.34 hectares (2009) to 929,117.65 hectares (2021), a 4% reduction.
    • Deforestation was more pronounced in non-forest areas, particularly in the Other Land Use (APL) zones, accounting for 62% of total deforestation. Forest cover outside forest zones decreased by 55% during the study period, dropping from 104,246.76 hectares in 2009 to 47,235.05 hectares in 2021.

    Challenges: Although reforestation efforts covered 25,137 hectares (19,926 hectares in forest zones), they remain insufficient to counteract the rapid rate of deforestation. Effective land management and environmental rehabilitation are urgently needed to minimize long-term impacts.
